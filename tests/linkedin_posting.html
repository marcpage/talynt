      Note: By applying to this position you will have an opportunity to share your preferred working location from the following: Reston, VA, USA; Austell, GA, USA; Austin, TX, USA; Papillion, NE, USA; New Albany, OH, USA; Midlothian, TX, USA; Pryor Creek, OK 74361, USA; Bridgeport, AL, USA.Minimum qualifications:

      • Bachelor's degree in Engineering, Computer Science, or equivalent practical experience.
      • 10 years of experience with mission critical facilities such as data centers, power plants, hospitals, or a similar role.
      • Experience designing, commissioning, and operating PLC/SCADA systems, from client/server HMIs, historians, and instrumentation and input/output interfaces.

      Preferred qualifications:

      • Experience as a controls engineering lead for systems integration in design or construction capacity.
      • Knowledge of multiple PLC/SCADA packages as applied to critical cooling and/or power systems.
      • Knowledge of controls or process optimization for mission critical facility systems.
      • Knowledge of data centers electrical infrastructure systems.
      • Ability to work in teams and within a matrix organization.
      • Ability to travel domestically and internationally up to 25% of the time as needed.

      About The Job

      Our thirst for technology is a part of everything we do. The Data Center Engineering team takes the physical design of our data centers into the future. Our lab mirrors a research and development department -- cutting-edge strategies are born, tested and tested again. Along with a team of great minds, you take on complex topics like how we use power or how to run state-of-the-art, environmentally-friendly facilities. You're a visionary who optimizes for efficiencies and never stops seeking improvements -- even small changes that can make a huge impact. You generate ideas, communicate recommendations to senior-level executives and drive implementation alongside facilities technicians.

      With your technical expertise, you ensure compliance with codes and standards, develop infrastructure improvements and serve as an expert in your specialty (e.g., cooling, electrical).

      Behind everything our users see online is the architecture built by the Technical Infrastructure team to keep it running. From developing and maintaining our data centers to building the next generation of Google platforms, we make Google's product portfolio possible. We're proud to be our engineers' engineers and love voiding warranties by taking things apart so we can rebuild them. We keep our networks up and running, ensuring our users have the best and fastest experience possible.

      Responsibilities

      • Collaborate with teams to develop data center monitoring and control system designs (basis of design, constructability, infrastructure expansions and retrofits).
      • Manage all control system engineering issues during design, bidding, implementation, and testing. Identify and resolve issues raised by cross-functional stakeholders.
      • Collaborate across disciplines and functions (mechanical, electrical, networking, project management, operations) and understand interrelated issues. Maintain internal design specifications, drawings and standards to the latest configurations.
      • Provide technical support to Operations and Project Managers (PM) in resolving all of the PMs related issues.
      • Develop and maintain functional relationships across multidisciplinary teams to anticipate future controls and monitoring design requirements.
